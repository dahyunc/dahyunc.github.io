Hello! I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Politics at Princeton University, where I am affiliated with the Data-Driven Social Science Initiative (DDSS), the Niehaus Center for Globalization and Governance (NCGG), and the Program for Quantitative and Analytical Political Science (Q-APS). My research interests include the political economy of regulation, information politics, formal and quantitative methodology. With a focus on environmental and trade regulations, my dissertation examines the strategic use of information by business interests.

The other stream of my research is related to computational social science. I use machine learning and applied statistical methods to quantify abstract concepts in social science and to evaluate measurement quality.
