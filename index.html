I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Politics at Princeton University. One stream of my research examines the political economy of information. Focusing on environmental regulations, trade and financial policies, I analyze the strategic use of information by business interests and empirically demonstrate their influence on regulation politics.

The other stream of my research addresses measurement problems in political science. I use machine learning and applied statistical methods to quantify the concepts in political science and improve the quality of measurement.
